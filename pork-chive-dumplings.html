Dumplings with Crispy Skirt and Dipping Sauce

Pork and Chive Dumplings

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork (at least 20% fat)
  • 1 large bunch Chinese chives, chopped (about 1/2 cup)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp shaoxing wine
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp MSG (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 package dumpling wrappers
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (for cooking)

Pork and Chive Dumplings - Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, chives, garlic, and ginger
  2. Season the mixture with salt, white pepper, sugar, light soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, MSG, and sesame oil
  3. Mix with your hands in a clockwise motion until the ingredients are thoroughly incorporated and you see streaks of protein on the edge of the bowl
  4. Taste your filling before wrapping by cooking 1 tbsp in the microwave for 30 seconds to 1 minute and adjusting seasoning if needed
  5. Place 1 tbsp of filling in the center of your dumpling wrapper
  6. Wet the edges with water, then pinch together one end to end
  7. Use your thumbs to pleat from the center to the edge of one side to seal the dumpling
  8. Repeat on the other side from the center to create your crescent dumpling shape

Crispy Dumpling Skirt Mixture

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup neutral oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 1.5 tbsp flour
  • 1.5 tbsp corn starch
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Cooking with Crispy Skirt - Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix together 1/4 cup neutral oil, 1 cup water, 1.5 tbsp flour, 1.5 tbsp corn starch, and 1/2 tsp salt
  2. Stir the mixture before pouring to redistribute the starch that settles to the bottom
  3. Pan fry your dumplings in 1 tbsp of oil for 2-3 minutes over medium high heat using a nonstick pan
  4. Once they are golden brown, add about 1 cup of the mixture (enough to come up to 1/4 way up the dumpling) to the pan and cover
  5. If cooking frozen dumplings, add more of the mixture to come up to 1/3 of the dumpling
  6. Cover and steam the dumplings for 5-6 minutes or until the water has evaporated and the residual oil is left
  7. Remove the lid. The skirt will form and become golden brown
  8. Watch this carefully to ensure it does not burn - this will happen quickly!
  9. Turn off the heat and carefully loosen the dumplings and skirt from the pan with a spatula
  10. Alternatively, place a plate large enough to cover the entire width of the pan and carefully invert the dumplings onto the plate

Dumpling Dipping Sauce

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Chinese black vinegar (Chinkiang vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp chili oil
  • 1/2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 scallion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 tsp sugar (optional)

Dumpling Dipping Sauce - Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, mix together 1 tbsp soy sauce
  2. Add 1 tbsp Chinese black vinegar (Chinkiang vinegar)
  3. Add 1 tbsp chili oil
  4. Add 1/2 tbsp sesame seeds
  5. Add 1 finely chopped scallion
  6. Add 1/4 tsp sugar if you prefer it slightly sweeter
  7. Stir all ingredients together until well combined
  8. Serve with your dumplings and enjoy!

Sauce Customization

Adjust to Your Preference

  • For more saltiness - add more soy sauce
  • For spicier sauce - add more chili oil
  • For more tangy/sour flavor - add more Chinese black vinegar
  • For sweeter sauce - add more sugar

Freezing and Storage

Tips

  • Once you've folded your Pork and Chive Dumplings, take a baking sheet and line it with parchment paper
  • Lay the dumplings on the baking sheet in a single layer with room between each so they do not stick together
  • Place the baking sheet with the dumplings in the freezer for AT LEAST one hour
  • After one hour, transfer them into a freezer safe bag (they can be laid on top of each other and will not freeze together)
  • Store the dipping sauce in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week
  • Use a nonstick pan appropriately sized for the number of dumplings you are cooking
  • If you don't have corn starch, you can use potato starch or rice flour for the dumpling skirt
