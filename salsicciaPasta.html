Creamy pasta with salsiccia, mascarpone & tomato

Ingredients

  • 500g pasta
  • 300g salsiccia
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 red paprika
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 handful of basilica
  • 1 can of chopped tomatoes
  • 200g mascarpone
  • 1.5dl white wine (can be replaced with bouillon)
  • 50g parmesan
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper
  • Oil

Instructions

  1. Heat a little oil in a pan and squeeze the sausage meat into the pan in large chunks.
  2. Sausages contain quite a lot of fat, so start by heating just a tablespoon of oil â€“ you can always add more.
  3. Fry the meat on a high heat for 5-8 minutes.
  4. If they release a lot of fat, I prefer to pour some off, but that's up to you.
  5. Add the onion, garlic, and bell pepper, season with a little salt and pepper, and fry for a few minutes before adding the tomato paste.
  6. Pour in the white wine and let it reduce a little, then add the tomatoes, Parmesan, basil, and mascarpone. Save a little basil to stir in just before serving, so it retains its nice color.
  7. Let the sauce simmer over high heat for about 10 minutes, while cooking the pasta as described on the package.
  8. Save a little pasta water before draining, in case you need to adjust the consistency.
  9. Season the sauce with salt and pepper, toss it with the drained pasta, and adjust the consistency with a little pasta water if necessary.
  10. Serve topped with grated Parmesan, freshly ground pepper, and fresh basil if desired.
